Imploding TikTok

Samuel Robson Implosion Project  ·  Joe Dumit, 2014  ·  14 Dimensions

The artifact is TikTok: a short-form video platform owned by ByteDance through which content reaches over a billion users via the algorithmic For You Page.

Drag cards to reorder.
01
Labor
Creators film, edit, and caption for little or no pay. The Creator Fund paid $0.02 to $0.04 per 1,000 views.1 Content moderators are outsourced to contractors in Kenya and the Philippines. A former Nairobi moderator sued ByteDance and its contractor Majorel after being diagnosed with PTSD, describing no meaningful psychiatric support.2 It is advertising revenue, sold through TikTok's commercial partnerships, that makes the free platform viable at all.
Confidence High on commercial operations from direct work experience on TikTok's Small Business Strategy team. Moderate on moderation infrastructure.
02
Professional & Epistemic
How the recommendation system works is largely unknowable from outside ByteDance. Academic API access is tightly restricted, and public understanding depends on leaks and court filings. Advertisers are told the algorithm rewards quality, a simplification that serves sales more than it describes the system.3
Confidence Moderate. Algorithmic explanations encountered through direct work experience were necessarily simplified for commercial audiences.
03
Material
US user data sits on Oracle Cloud infrastructure under Project Texas, ByteDance's $1.5 billion national security concession to the US government.4,5 Server hardware requires cobalt from the DRC and rare earths from Inner Mongolia. TikTok's data centre energy footprint is not publicly disclosed.
Confidence Moderate. Infrastructure partially documented in regulatory filings. Energy data unavailable.
04
Technological
The For You Page ranks content on implicit signals: watch time, replays, shares, rather than social connections. The architecture likely follows a two-tower neural network model, inferred from published ByteDance research rather than confirmed internally. Douyin runs on separate infrastructure under different content rules.
Confidence High on product architecture from direct work experience with TikTok ad technology teams. Moderate on internal specifics, which are inferred.
05
Context & Situatedness
TikTok is typically encountered on a vertical screen with sound on, in the hour before sleep. It is a sound-first platform by design, inheriting Musical.ly's lip-sync culture. The infinite scroll has no built-in stopping point, which is a product decision rather than a technical constraint.
Confidence High. Directly observable and consistent with direct work experience of the platform.
06
Political
The 2024 Protecting Americans from Foreign Adversary Controlled Applications Act required ByteDance to divest TikTok or face a ban. The app went dark briefly in January 2025.6 CFIUS scrutiny dates to 2019. India banned the platform outright in 2020. EU Digital Services Act obligations now apply across European markets.
Confidence High. Based on public legislative records and court filings.
07
Economic
Revenue runs on a real-time CPM auction in which paid and organic content compete invisibly. ByteDance reported over $110 billion in revenue in 2023, with TikTok and Douyin as the primary contributors.7 The pitch to small businesses was that ads feel native to the feed, a claim that only holds if organic content stays compelling enough to hold attention.
Confidence High. Revenue from Bloomberg. Commercial model and SMB pitch from direct work experience on TikTok's Small Business Strategy team.
08
Textual
TikTok carries text it did not author: ASR captions, hashtags as algorithmic signals, and a comment section that operates as its own performative genre. The platform's journalistic record is anchored by the BuzzFeed News internal recordings8 and The Guardian's 2019 moderation guidelines leak.9
Confidence High. Directly observable. Sources cited and publicly available.
09
Bodily & Organic
The 9:16 format fits the resting grip of a phone-holding hand. Content is engineered to hook within two seconds because completion rate drives ranking. Research associates heavy use with anxiety, depression, and disrupted sleep in adolescents.10
Confidence Moderate. Design rationale inferred from product behaviour. Health outcomes from peer-reviewed literature.
10
Historical
Musical.ly was founded in Shanghai in 2014, acquired by ByteDance in 2017, and relaunched as TikTok in 2018.11 COVID-19 lockdowns drove 315 million downloads in Q1 2020, a quarterly record at the time.12 Leaked 2019 moderation documents showed instructions to suppress content about Tiananmen Square, Tibetan independence, and Falun Gong.9
Confidence High. Corporate history and figures well documented. Moderation leak via The Guardian.
11
Particle
TikTok decomposes into: the video file; the sound, which circulates independently across videos and is the primary viral vector; the recommendation event that matches content to user; and the interaction affordances (like, stitch, duet) whose signals feed back into the model.
Confidence High. Directly observable product structure, consistent with direct work experience of the platform.
12
Educational
"LearnTok" emerged without the platform intending it, with doctors, historians, and scientists building large audiences. The problem is that the same ranking logic governs health misinformation and careful expertise alike. Confident delivery routinely outperforms accuracy.
Confidence Moderate. Trend widely documented. Algorithmic parity between accuracy and misinformation is inferred from known ranking signals.
13
Mythological
The founding myth is democratic reach: anyone can go viral without an existing audience. This is true often enough to sustain a large unpaid creator base. The algorithm is anthropomorphised as an oracle. In US political culture, TikTok has become a vessel for surveillance anxieties that exceed the documented evidence.
Confidence High on creator mythology from direct work experience. Moderate on surveillance claims.
14
Symbolic
TikTok is simultaneously a symbol of democratised creative reach and of Chinese technological ambition at the centre of everyday American life. Creators type "fyp" in captions believing it signals the algorithm toward wider distribution. It does not work that way. The ritual persists because the system is opaque.
Confidence Moderate. Cultural observation rather than empirical measurement.
